The Cost of Governance — Part 1: Federal Outlays and Obligations

  • Total Federal Outlays: $7.0 trillion — includes defense, Medicare, Social Security, and all discretionary spending
  • Federal Revenues: $5.2 trillion — mostly from income taxes, payroll taxes, and corporate taxes
  • Annual Deficit: $1.9 trillion — the gap between what the government spends and what it collects
  • Debt Held by the Public: $30.1 trillion — owed to investors, banks, and foreign governments
  • Total Federal Debt: $39.0 trillion — includes intragovernmental holdings like Social Security trust funds
  • Debt Ceiling: Raised to $41.1 trillion on July 4, 2025 — a temporary fix, not a structural solution
  • Interest Payments: $1.02 trillion — now one of the largest federal expenses, rivaling Medicare and Defense
