  • The Earlier You Start
    Starting at 25 with $100/month can grow to $250K by retirement. Wait until 50, and you’ll need $500/month to reach the same goal. At 60, it’s $1,000/month. Time is the multiplier.

    • $25/month → ~$63K by retirement • $50/month → ~$125K • $100/month → ~$250K
    These estimates assume a 7% annual return over 40 years. The earlier you start, the more time compounds your effort.
  • Is Social Security Enough?
    When you're young, you pay Social Security taxes and barely think twice. It feels automatic—just another line on your paycheck. But here's the truth: Social Security was never designed to be a full retirement fund. It was built as a safety net, not a landing pad.

    Today, most retirees need at least two other sources of income to maintain stability—whether it's savings, pensions, or investments. Social Security alone leaves gaps.

    And if the system falters—as some forecasts warn it could within the next three years—it won’t just be a policy failure. It’ll be a national reckoning. The fallout would be so severe, not a single member of Congress would survive the political blowback. This is one of the few institutions they’ll fight tooth and nail to preserve.
  • Your 401K

    Why 401(k)s Matter: In past decades, many workers received guaranteed retirement pay through pensions. Today, those pensions are rare. For most people, their 401(k) will be their retirement paycheck. Participating early—and consistently—is the best way to build long-term security.

    Tip 3: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. That’s free money—don’t leave it on the table.

    Where to start: Begin with 5–7% of your salary if you're in your 20s. If that feels tight, start at 3% and increase by 1% each year. Most plans let you automate this bump.

    Why it works: Gradual increases timed with raises or bonuses won’t sting your budget, but they’ll compound dramatically over time. A 25-year-old contributing 5% with a 50% match could retire with over $450K—even without maxing out.

    Bonus clarity: Many employers match 50¢ on the dollar up to 6% of your salary. That’s a 50% return before the market even kicks in.
