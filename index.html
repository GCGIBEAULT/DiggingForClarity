The Earlier You Start
Starting at 25 with $100/month can grow to $250K by retirement. Wait until 50, and you’ll need $500/month to reach the same goal. At 60, it’s $1,000/month. Time is the multiplier.

Is Social Security Enough?
When you’re young, you pay Social Security taxes and barely think twice. It feels automatic—just another line on your paycheck. But here’s the truth: Social Security was never designed to be a full retirement fund. It was built as a safety net, not a landing pad.

Today, most retirees need at least two other sources of income to maintain stability—whether it’s savings, pensions, or investments. Social Security alone leaves gaps.

And if the system falters—as some forecasts warn it could within the next three years—it won’t just be a policy failure. It’ll be a national reckoning. The fallout would be so severe, not a single member of Congress would survive the political blowback. This is one of the few institutions they’ll fight tooth and nail to preserve.
